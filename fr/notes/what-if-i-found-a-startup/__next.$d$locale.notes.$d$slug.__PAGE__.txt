17:T1873,<p>If I founded a software company tomorrow, these are the principles I would encode into its DNA before writing a single line of code.</p>
<p>Before founding anything, the prerequisite is knowing why it should exist: establish <strong>the mission</strong>. Everything below is downstream of that one answer.</p>
<figure class="blog-figure blog-photo-panorama">
  <img src="https://nguyenlephong.github.io/dom-pub/icdn/notes/what-if-i-found-a-startup/mission-culture-table.webp" width="1600" height="900" loading="lazy" decoding="async" alt="A small Vietnamese founding team arranges blank cards and product tools around a warm office table before defining a startup culture." />
  <figcaption>Before there is a company to build, the first work is making the mission, trade-offs, and operating habits visible.</figcaption>
</figure>
<h2 id="culture-values">Culture &amp; values</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Default to open</strong> — share work early and often; radical transparency in decisions; learn in public, documenting misses and learnings openly.</li>
<li><strong>Strong opinions, loosely held</strong> — advocate fiercely, but pivot quickly with new data.</li>
<li><strong>Ship daily</strong> — bias toward action and visible progress over perfect planning.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="decisions">Decision-making</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Type 1 vs. Type 2 decisions</strong> — reversible decisions made quickly by individuals; irreversible ones with group input (Jeff Bezos&rsquo;s &ldquo;one-way&rdquo; vs. &ldquo;two-way&rdquo; doors).</li>
<li><strong>Disagree and commit</strong> — one decision made, full alignment regardless of initial positions.</li>
<li><strong>Data-informed, not data-driven</strong> — metrics as input, but <em>trust intuition</em> for innovation.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="cultural-principles">Cultural principles</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>No brilliant jerks</strong> — competence never excuses poor collaboration.</li>
<li><strong>Work-life integration</strong>, not balance — flexible boundaries, results over hours.</li>
<li><strong>Celebration of craft</strong> — pride in elegant solutions and beautiful code.</li>
<li><strong>&ldquo;Yes, and&hellip;&rdquo;</strong> culture — build on ideas rather than shooting them down.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="team-hiring">Team &amp; hiring</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Builder-first mentality</strong> — every hire should be able to ship something independently within the first week.</li>
<li><strong>Cultural add, not cultural fit</strong> — founding hires should add net-positive to the culture, not mirror it. <em>(<a href="https://x.com/zfellows/status/1824443390244594000?s=46">Reference</a>.)</em></li>
</ul>
<figure class="blog-figure blog-photo-figure">
  <img src="https://nguyenlephong.github.io/dom-pub/icdn/notes/what-if-i-found-a-startup/builder-first-project-review.webp" width="1440" height="960" loading="lazy" decoding="async" alt="Three Vietnamese teammates review a small prototype demo at a wooden startup table with laptops, blank notes, and careful attention." />
  <figcaption>Builder-first hiring is easier to judge when people can show how they move unclear work into a usable first version.</figcaption>
</figure>
<h3 id="competencies">Core competencies</h3>
<p>Assessed in interviews and contract-to-hire projects:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>AI utilization</strong> is expected — leverage AI into the value you produce.</li>
<li><strong>Excellent writing</strong> — I don&rsquo;t believe someone can be a good thinker and a bad writer.</li>
<li><strong>Fluent English</strong>, especially written — a proxy that proves you are an intrinsic learner.</li>
<li><strong>Autonomy and high agency</strong> — they spot the problem before I do, then solve it without being asked.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="composition">Team composition</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Small pods</strong> (2–3 people) with full ownership of specific products or features.</li>
<li><strong>No middle management</strong> — flat hierarchy, direct communication.</li>
<li><strong>Rotating leadership</strong> — different people lead different initiatives by expertise.</li>
<li><strong>Two-pizza teams</strong> — six to eight people is the sweet spot.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="growth">Growth</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Slow hiring</strong> — the first ten hires over 12–18 months, each one raising the bar.</li>
<li><strong>Contract-to-hire</strong> — test compatibility through project work first; interviews are capricious, projects are the best litmus test. (Case study: Kepano became Obsidian&rsquo;s CEO through contractor work.) The bar: I have to be <strong>amazed</strong> by the output to consider hiring.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="performance">Performance evaluation</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Glory list</strong> — each member keeps their own list of wins, revised together in 1-1s, as the primary input for evaluation. No predefined OKRs or KPIs: plans and directions pivot all the time, rendering those frameworks volatile and inefficient.</li>
</ul>
<figure class="blog-figure blog-photo-figure">
  <img src="https://nguyenlephong.github.io/dom-pub/icdn/notes/what-if-i-found-a-startup/glory-list-communication-rhythm.webp" width="1440" height="960" loading="lazy" decoding="async" alt="Two Vietnamese teammates review blank win cards and abstract message shapes during a calm one-on-one conversation." />
  <figcaption>A glory list and a clear mention rhythm turn recognition and communication into a shared practice, not a constant interruption.</figcaption>
</figure>
<h2 id="tools-comms">Tools &amp; communication</h2>
<ul>
<li>Notion as the company knowledge base; Slack as the main channel — no internal email.</li>
<li>Treat <strong>mentions</strong> as a mail inbox: process them on a cadence, address each one. Treat non-mentions as a broadcast bulletin board — read them as news.</li>
<li>If a message <em>should</em> be seen by someone, mention their name. If it <em>must</em> be seen, write <code>ackp</code> (&ldquo;acknowledge please&rdquo;).</li>
<li>Replying when you see a should/must message is decent etiquette — especially when an action hangs on it.</li>
<li>If synchronous communication or immediate action is required, tap a shoulder or call. Synchronize sparingly.</li>
