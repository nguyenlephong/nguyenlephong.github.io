4:T5b3,<p>A common failure mode in learning where we accumulate knowledge without progressing through the <span class="private-link">KUB - Three stages of comprehension</span> framework.</p>
<h3>Symptoms</h3>
<ul>
<li>Constantly reading new books/articles without applying insights</li>
<li>Feeling knowledgeable but unable to demonstrate competence</li>
<li>Collecting information as a form of productive procrastination</li>
<li>Mistaking recognition for understanding</li>
</ul>
<h3>Why it happens</h3>
<ul>
<li>Information feels like progress (dopamine from novelty)</li>
<li>Action requires vulnerability and risk of failure</li>
<li>Knowledge hoarding feels safer than practice</li>
<li>Modern information abundance makes consumption endless</li>
</ul>
<h3>Breaking the trap</h3>
<ul>
<li>Set implementation intentions before consuming content</li>
<li>Practice "just-in-time" learning vs "just-in-case" learning</li>
<li>Create friction for consumption, remove friction for practice</li>
<li>Track actions taken, not information consumed</li>
</ul>
<h3>Related concepts</h3>
<ul>
<li><span class="private-link">Why thinking?</span> - Purposeful vs busy thinking</li>
<li><span class="private-link">States of Mind</span> - Balance contemplation with flow/action</li>
</ul>
