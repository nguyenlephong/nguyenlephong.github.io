15:T9a9,<p>Most of the time, the answer is already in you. Deep down you know what is right to do — the honest next move, the thing you keep circling back to. You just don&rsquo;t usually listen, because listening means admitting the answer is inconvenient. So when you genuinely don&rsquo;t know what to do now, stop interrogating your todo list and listen instead for:</p>
<figure class="blog-figure blog-photo-panorama">
  <img src="https://nguyenlephong.github.io/dom-pub/icdn/notes/the-heuristics-for-what-to-do-now/listening-for-the-next-signal.webp" width="1600" height="900" loading="lazy" decoding="async" alt="A Vietnamese adult pauses at a warm desk with a blank notebook, cards, a closed laptop, tea, and a face-down phone before choosing the next action." />
  <figcaption>The next honest move often appears when the small signals are finally allowed onto the table.</figcaption>
</figure>
<ul>
<li>The things you feel <strong>resistance</strong> toward — the ones you have always <em>avoided</em>. The friction itself is the signal; we rarely flinch away from what doesn&rsquo;t matter.</li>
<li>The things that break your <strong>balance</strong> and drop you into a <em>scarcity</em> mentality. The moves that make you feel there isn&rsquo;t enough are usually the ones forcing you to grow past your current size.</li>
<li>The things you <strong>dream</strong> of but don&rsquo;t believe you can reach. The quiet ambition you dismiss as unrealistic is often the most accurate map of where you actually want to go.</li>
</ul>
<p>Notice that all three point toward discomfort, not away from it. That is not a coincidence; it is the method. Think of someone who has been meaning to start writing for years but keeps &ldquo;not finding the time&rdquo; — the resistance, the fear it won&rsquo;t be good enough, the half-buried dream are all pointing the same way. Those are <em>the North</em> of your compass.</p>
