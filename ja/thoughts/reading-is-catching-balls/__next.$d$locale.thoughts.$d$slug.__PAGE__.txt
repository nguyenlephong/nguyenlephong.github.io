4:T4d1,<p>An analogy on book reading from <span class="private-link">How to Read a Book - Adler &#x26; Doren</span>.</p>
<p>It greatly expresses the relation between <strong>writer</strong> and <strong>reader</strong> is remarkably similar to the relation betwen <strong>pitcher</strong> and <strong>catcher</strong> in baseball.
Both activities of <em>pitching-writing</em> and <em>catching-reading</em> are <strong>active</strong>, and <strong>demanding efforts</strong>. There is no such thing as <em>passive catching</em> or <em>passive reading</em>.</p>
<p>The whole activitiy success is how well they cooporate. It's not a win of the pitcher-writer throw a <strong>difficult ball</strong> but the catcher-reader unable to catch it. Neither if the pitcher-writer throw an <strong>easy ball</strong> but the ball won't pass the batter (in the case of reading, the batter would be boredom).</p>
<p>The pitcher-writer and catcher-reader are two persons with different level of expertise. Careerwise speaking, they always practice to level up. But it won't be optimal of the <strong>disparity in levels</strong> are too far.
