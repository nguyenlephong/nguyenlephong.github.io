4:T134e,<p>If I founded a software company tomorrow, these are the principles I'd encode into its DNA before writing a single line of code.</p>
<p>Before founding a company, the prerequisite is knowing why it should exist: establish <strong>the mission</strong>.</p>
<h2>Culture &#x26; Values</h2>
<h3>Core Values</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Default to Open</strong> - Share work early and often. Radical transparency in decisions.
<ul>
<li>Learn in public - Document failures and learnings openly</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Strong opinions, loosely held</strong> - Advocate fiercely, but pivot quickly with new data.</li>
<li><strong>"Ship daily"</strong> - Bias toward action and visible progress over perfect planning</li>
</ul>
<h3>Decision-Making Framework</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Type 1 vs. Type 2 decisions</strong> - reversible decisions made quickly by individuals, and irreversible ones with group input
(Jeff Bezos mentioned this as "one-way" vs. "two-way" doors.)</li>
<li><strong>Disagree and commit</strong> - One decision made, full team alignment regardless of initial position</li>
<li><strong>Data-informed, not data-driven</strong> - Use metrics as input but <em>trust intuition</em> for innovation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Cultural Principles</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>No brilliant jerks</strong> - Competence never excuses poor collaboration</li>
<li><strong>Work-life integration</strong> not balance - Flexible boundaries, results over hours</li>
<li><strong>Celebration of craft</strong> - Take pride in elegant solutions and beautiful code</li>
<li><strong>Yes, and...</strong> culture - Build on ideas rather than shooting them down</li>
</ul>
<h2>Team Structure &#x26; Hiring</h2>
<h3>Hiring Philosophy</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Builder-first mentality</strong> - Every hire should be capable of shipping something independently within the first week.</li>
<li><strong>Cultural add, not cultural fit</strong> - (for founding hires) The person should add net positive to the company culture.</li>
</ul>
<p>References: https://x.com/zfellows/status/1824443390244594000?s=46</p>
<h3>Core Competencies</h3>
<p>These criteria are assessed in interviews and contract-to-hire projects:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>AI utilization skill</strong> is expected - Leverage and augment AI usage into your produced values</li>
<li><strong>Excellent writer</strong> - I don't believe someone can be a good thinker and a bad writer</li>
<li><strong>Fluent in English</strong>, especially in writing - This is a proxy skill requirement, it proves that you are an intrinsic learner</li>
<li><strong>Autonomous and high agency</strong> - They spot out the problem before me. They come up with solution and execute it even if I don't ask for it</li>
</ul>
<h3>Team Composition Strategy</h3>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Small pods</strong> (2-3 people max) - with full ownership of specific products/features</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>No middle management</strong> - flat hierarchy with direct communication</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Rotating leadership</strong> - Different people lead different initiatives based on expertise</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Two-pizza teams</strong> - 6-8 people on a team is the sweet spot.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3>Growth Plans</h3>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Slow hiring</strong> - the first 10 hires over 12-18 months. Each one raising the bar.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Contract-to-hire</strong> - Test compatibility through Project work first. Interviews are capricious, projects are the best litmus tests.</p>
<ul>
<li>Case study: Kepano got hired as Obsidian CEO through contractor works.</li>
<li>Evaluation: I have to be <strong>amazed</strong> by the output to consider hiring the person.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2>Performance Evaluation Framework</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Glory list</strong> - Each member is obliged to jot down their list, revised with me (on 1-1). The list will be the primary input factor for performance evaluation. That means no predefined OKR / KPI. Planning and direction get pivot all the times, rendering those performance framework volatile and inefficient.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Tool stack</h2>
<ul>
<li>Notion as primary company knowledge base</li>
<li>Slack as the main means of communication - No internal emails</li>
</ul>
<h2>Communication Protocol</h2>
<ul>
<li>Treat mentioned chat messages as mail inbox. Process them in a cadence, address each of them accordingly. Treat non-mentioned as broadcast bulletin board information, read them as news.</li>
<li>If a message <em>should</em> be seen by a person, mention their name</li>
<li>If a message <em>must</em> be seen by a person, write <code>ackp</code> (acknowledge please) in the message.</li>
<li>Replying to either should or must level when you saw it is a decent etiquette to maintain, especially when it has an action to take.</li>
<li>If synchronous communication or immediate action is required, pat the other back or use phone call. Synchronize sparingly.</li>
