16:T781,<p>If I could send a short note back to myself in my twenties, it would not be a strategy. It would be a handful of things I had to learn the slow way, written plainly enough to actually land.</p>
<ol>
<li>Develop your <strong>system</strong>. Don&rsquo;t rely on willpower or mood to carry you — build the scaffolding that carries you when both run out.</li>
<li>Perfectionism is harmful; life is about moving forward. A finished, imperfect thing teaches you more than a perfect thing you never ship.</li>
<li>The most precious lessons are in your everyday life — not in grand events, but in the ordinary days you are tempted to overlook.</li>
<li>Your ego is not you. The voice defending your image is a passenger, not the driver.</li>
<li>Don&rsquo;t stereotype yourself. The labels you accept quietly become the ceiling you live under.</li>
<li>Tolerate imperfect attempts — leave room for others and for yourself to learn. A space where mistakes are survivable is a space where people actually grow.</li>
<li>You cannot calculate it all. Sometimes you make a decision with little information at hand, and you take responsibility for it.</li>
<li>Character is proven in wars, not in peace. Comfort flatters everyone; it is pressure that shows you who you really are.</li>
</ol>

<li>I wish I had stayed in touch with my friends.</li>
