4:Tad6,<p>Comprehensively composed of all the sub-systems for how I run most aspects of my life where a rule-engine system is employable.</p>
<p>This system's overall implementation strongly prefers using apps. The reason being to leverage app <strong>notifications</strong> along with the <span class="private-link">First-screen Principle</span> to alleviate the mental tax of decision making and the constant anxiety of “doing the right thing”.</p>
<p>Above all of the sub-systems is the mindset that govern their execution. The current model to adhere to is <span class="private-link">States of Mind</span>.</p>
<h3>Driver Todo System</h3>
<p>You take the driver seat.</p>
<p>App: <strong>Things</strong></p>
<p>Every task and project is an opportunity to pick up. They have a <em>positive</em> desirable outcome to pursue. Every progress pushes your life forward.</p>
<p>The performance is not preallocated, not time-scheduled. The <strong>mentality</strong> when executing the task backlog when you are in the driver sea is with high urgency: "could I done this with the minimal time?", or "could I done the best job for this in 30 minutes?". <em>The faster I accomplish the outcome, the better</em>.</p>
<p>Excluded of routine, scheduled tasks. This goal-todo system contains opportunities, not obligations (there's a YouTube video from Ali Abdaal regarding this).</p>
<p>Related: <span class="private-link">Healthy todo backlog</span></p>
<h3>Accountability Todo System</h3>
<p>Non-negotiable, execute with no wasted mental cycle, that is the beauty of the system.</p>
<p>Apps: <strong>Calendar</strong> + <strong>Reminders</strong> + <strong>Streaks</strong></p>
<p>The system manage <em>obligations</em> and <em>routines</em> that I just mindlessly do. Most of them are time-scheduled or have a very clear do-able context.</p>
<p>The "<strong>when to do it</strong>" rule:</p>
<ul>
<li>When it is due, evening for date-scheduled, when it appears for datetime-scheduled.</li>
<li>In context (location, equipment). There's only a single context now:
<ul>
<li><em>Groceries</em>: when I'm at a grocery store.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h3>Reflection Cadence</h3>
<p>Reflection cadence is the system <strong>dynamic</strong> of evolution of self-stabilizing.  Reflection sessions are performed routinely, on recurrent schedules.</p>
<p>There are three cycles, <strong>daily</strong>, <strong>weekly</strong> and <strong>monthly</strong>. They are scheduled with noted details within the Reminders app:</p>
<ul>
<li><span class="private-link">The Day</span></li>
<li><span class="private-link">Weekly Reflection</span></li>
<li><span class="private-link">Monthly Reflection</span></li>
</ul>
